# The Toolbox Grew an Arcade

Okay, so. The Toolbox has always been the place you come when you need help: a resume, a meal plan, a road trip that doesn't fall apart on day two. Structure with a personality. That's the whole deal.

This one's not that. This one doesn't need anything from you. This one just wants you to have a good time for ten minutes, and it happens to star two extremely famous cats.

**Glee-fully Chai Chasers** is a free browser game built for one person, then opened up for everyone to enjoy. Real cascading reels. A meter that climbs toward something. A wheel you spin. A legendary symbol so rare that landing it is basically a personality trait once it happens to you. It all runs on fictional Glee-coins, and if your balance ever gets low, a very specific co-pilot finds more under the couch and hands them over with a shrug.

## Meet the cast

You already know **Joey and Phoebe** if you've spent any time in this ecosystem, but here they get to be the main characters. Phoebe shows up for literally any treat, no judgment, she's a queen and she knows it. Joey is pickier, obviously, because Joey has standards, and Joey only shows up for his specific treat. If the jar's empty when he wanders by, he looks at the board, looks at you, and leaves. That's canon. That's just who he is.

There's an iced chai tumbler, jewel-toned and mermaid-patterned, doing top-symbol duty on the reels, because if you know, you know: this house does not do hot chai. There's a number twelve doing a lot of quiet, sacred work throughout the whole thing. There's a legendary rainbow-butterfly bonus called the UniGlee that shows up roughly once every four hundred spins and turns the entire screen violet when it does. You'll know it when you see it. You'll probably yell.
